## Overall Impression

This is a genuinely well-considered marketing page — the copy, pacing, and typographic restraint show real design intent, and the belief-ladder ordering (safe → competent → easy to book) mostly holds section-by-section. The single biggest opportunity is tightening the details that undermine the "one clear action" promise the whole page is built around: a second CTA color splits the WhatsApp booking signal in two, there's no visible focus treatment for keyboard users, and the page's single most important sentence for an anxious visitor — the confidentiality guarantee — is buried three accordion-clicks deep in the FAQ instead of being surfaced where the anxiety actually peaks.

## What's Working

1. **The "Investigação de ISTs" service card** pairs the sensitive label directly with reassurance copy ("com total sigilo e acolhimento") in the same breath — precise emotional design at the single riskiest word on the page.
2. **Typographic restraint is real, not accidental.** The hero H1 is the only "big" statement on the page; every section title is deliberately a step down (`clamp(26-38px)` vs. `clamp(32-52px)`), and the CSS comments show this was a conscious choice, not luck.
3. **The FAQ accordion animates to real `scrollHeight`** rather than a guessed fixed max-height, enforces single-open, and one testimonial explicitly names patient anxiety ("Cheguei ansiosa e cheia de dúvidas...") — a strategic peer-permission placement for nervous first-time readers.

## Priority Issues

**[P1] The WhatsApp CTA renders in two different colors for the identical action.** The nav bar's "Agendar Consulta" uses `.btn-green` (teal) while every other instance of the same booking action — hero, serviços, como funciona, cta-final — uses `.btn-pink`. This splits the "this is the one thing to click" signal the whole page depends on into two competing colors, and fails Nielsen's Consistency heuristic directly.
**Why it matters:** the entire conversion strategy is "one repeated, unmissable action" — DESIGN.md's own Do's and Don'ts insist the WhatsApp CTA "must stay the single most prominent action" throughout. A second color for the same button dilutes exactly the signal the design system was built to protect.
**Fix:** switch the nav CTA to `.btn-pink` (or reserve teal exclusively for a genuinely different action, if one exists).
**Suggested command:** `/impeccable polish`

**[P1] No visible focus-state styling anywhere in style.css.** Buttons, nav links, the menu toggle, FAQ accordion triggers, and carousel arrows/dots all rely entirely on browser-default focus rings — untested against the pink/white/soft-surface palette.
**Why it matters:** this is an interaction-heavy page (accordion, carousel, mobile menu) with zero forms, so keyboard and screen-reader users depend entirely on focus visibility to operate it; PRODUCT.md commits to WCAG AA, and this is a direct, checkable gap against that commitment.
**Fix:** add an explicit, high-contrast `:focus-visible` treatment (e.g., a 2px pink outline with offset) applied site-wide to every interactive element.
**Suggested command:** `/impeccable audit`

**[P2] The confidentiality answer is buried three clicks deep in the FAQ, right after the page's highest-anxiety mention.** The services FAQ entry names "investigação de ISTs" in plain running text with no adjacent reassurance, and the direct privacy promise ("O atendimento é sigiloso?") sits at accordion position 3 — reachable only by opening two prior answers one at a time, with no way to scan all answers at once.
**Why it matters:** PRODUCT.md's belief ladder puts "safe and confidential" as the first rung a visitor must climb, specifically calling out IST-related anxiety as a design-critical case. Right now the page's single most load-bearing sentence for that visitor is the hardest one to reach.
**Fix:** promote the confidentiality FAQ item to position 1 or 2, or fold a short sigilo clause directly into the services FAQ answer where ISTs are named.
**Suggested command:** `/impeccable clarify`

**[P2] Orphaned/dead CSS misrepresents the shipped design.** `.hero-circle-1`/`.hero-circle-2` are commented in the source as the deliberate atmospheric counterpart to `.cta-final-glow` (bookending the page open/close) but were never inserted into the hero markup, so that intended symmetry doesn't exist as built. `.eyebrow` (a tracked-uppercase label class) and `.footer-social a.li:hover` (a LinkedIn hover treatment for an icon not present in the footer) are both unused. Separately, `privacidade.html`'s inline styles drift off the DESIGN.md scale (14px/15px/22px font sizes, 16px radius, a 1.7:1 type-hierarchy ratio) — confirmed by the deterministic scan.
**Why it matters:** dead code claiming an effect that isn't shipped is a trap for the next person editing this file, and the second page on the site is already off the documented design system.
**Fix:** either wire the hero circles into the hero markup for the intended open/close symmetry, or delete the dead rules; bring `privacidade.html`'s type scale onto the DESIGN.md ramp.
**Suggested command:** `/impeccable audit`

**[P3] `.info-value` (Localização info cards) is missing the `overflow-wrap: anywhere` rule that the footer already applies to the same long email string.** Inconsistent handling of identical text risks clipped/overflowing text on narrow (~320-360px) viewports.
**Fix:** add the matching `overflow-wrap: anywhere` rule to `.info-value`.
**Suggested command:** `/impeccable adapt`

## Persona Red Flags

**Jordan (Confused First-Timer):** Moves smoothly through hero → sobre → serviços with one consistent pink CTA, but stumbles at the header itself: the nav CTA is green while the section she's reading uses pink — a small but real "is this a different thing?" hesitation at the very first decision point on the page. She also only reaches "Como Funciona" (the plain-language process explainer she needs most) after passing two earlier CTA opportunities.

**Riley (Deliberate Stress Tester):** Tabbing through via keyboard finds no custom focus styling anywhere — a real, checkable gap on an interaction-heavy, form-free page (accordion, carousel, menu toggle). Resizing the viewport, the desktop nav collapses to hamburger at 768px, but the localização/footer grids don't reflow until 900px — an ~800px-wide tablet gets the mobile nav paired with a still-desktop-width two-column map layout, an inconsistent "which device tier am I in" experience.

**Casey (Distracted Mobile User):** Both `.float-card` trust badges over the hero photo ("Cuidado real" and "Ambiente seguro: Sigilo e acolhimento") are `display:none` below 768px — the two floating visual reinforcements of the safety belief-ladder rung are invisible on mobile, likely the majority of traffic for a WhatsApp-first local clinic. Casey gets only the plain benefits-list instead, a real loss of emphasis exactly where safety needs reinforcing.

**Ana (project-specific — 27, suspects an IST, browsing alone at night on her phone, ready to bounce at the first sign of exposure or judgment):** The pre-filled WhatsApp message stays generic ("gostaria de agendar uma consulta...") so she's never forced to type her actual reason before reaching a human — a genuine strength, as is the FAQ confirming no medical referral is needed. But "Investigação de ISTs" is stated plainly in a public-facing card grid with no softer entry point for someone worried about a passerby glancing at her screen; the confidentiality FAQ answer — the single most load-bearing sentence for her specifically — sits at accordion position 3; and no copy anywhere explicitly names and defuses the fear of judgment ("acolhimento"/"respeito" are adjacent concepts but don't say "sem julgamento" directly).

## Minor Observations

- `aria-expanded` is correctly implemented on both the FAQ buttons and the mobile menu toggle.
- The testimonial carousel autoplays at 6s, which may outrun reading time for the longest quote (hover-pause exists as a mitigation, but worth a manual QA pass).
- `og:image`/`twitter:image` point to real clinic photography, not stock — consistent with the brand's anti-stock-photo commitment.
- The map iframe's placeholder background (`var(--border)`) is a reasonable, unstated fallback while it loads.

## Questions to Consider

- If the confidentiality promise is the single highest-leverage sentence on this page for the target anxious visitor, why does it live three accordion-clicks deep instead of surfacing as an above-the-fold trust signal?
- Why does the identical "book via WhatsApp" action render in two different brand colors depending on which part of the page you're on?
- Given the hero's trust float-cards vanish entirely on mobile — the dominant device for this audience — has the safety message actually been validated as equally strong without them, or is mobile quietly getting a weaker version of the belief ladder's first rung?
